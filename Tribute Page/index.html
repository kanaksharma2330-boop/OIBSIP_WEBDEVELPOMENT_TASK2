Biography

Alan Turing was a British mathematician, logician, and computer scientist who is widely regarded as the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence. His groundbreaking ideas laid the foundation for modern computing.

During World War II, Turing worked at Bletchley Park where he played a crucial role in breaking the German Enigma code. His work significantly contributed to the Allied victory and helped shorten the war.

Turing introduced the concept of the "Turing Machine", a theoretical device that became the basis for the design of modern computers. His work continues to influence the fields of computer science, artificial intelligence, and cryptography.

Despite his enormous contributions, Turing faced discrimination during his lifetime. Today he is remembered as one of the most brilliant minds in the history of science and technology.
